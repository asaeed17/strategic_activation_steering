1.‚Ā† ‚Ā†You have no idea if your vectors mean anything
The pipeline extracts a "firmness" direction and immediately uses it to steer. There is zero verification that the extracted direction actually encodes firmness rather than some correlated artefact like response length or confidence in general. You could be steering the model to produce longer responses and winning negotiations because longer responses happen to contain more counter-arguments ‚ÄĒ nothing to do with firmness.

‚Äá2.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Your scoring breaks when targets don't overlap
score_deal() computes span = seller_target - buyer_target and divides by it. If targets overlap the span is negative and scores go above 1 or below 0. The function clamps them but the clamping masks the problem ‚ÄĒ you're silently producing garbage scores for a non-trivial fraction of games and your averages are wrong. Check how many scenarios in your current results have seller_target < buyer_target.

‚Äá3.‚Ā† ‚Ā†The steered agent is playing against itself
The same model generates both agents. The baseline agent sees the steered agent's unusually firm utterances and adapts to them in context. You are not measuring "does a firm agent beat a normal agent" ‚ÄĒ you are measuring "does a firm agent beat a normal agent that has already been influenced by the firm agent's previous turns." The two agents are not independent and your advantage scores are inflated as a result.

‚Äá4.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Your contrastive pairs are untested
negotiation_steering_pairs.json is the foundation of every vector in the pipeline but you have never checked whether the pairs actually separate cleanly in hidden space. If the positive and negative examples in a pair are not meaningfully distinct in the model's representation, the extracted direction is noise. You need to verify separation before trusting any downstream result.

‚Äá5.‚Ā† ‚Ā†You are searching over alpha without knowing what alpha does to the model
fast_search_steering.py treats alpha as a black-box hyperparameter to optimise. But you have no characterisation of what happens to the hidden states as alpha increases ‚ÄĒ at what point does the representation become incoherent, at what point does the probed concept saturate, and at what point are you just adding noise. You are optimising blind.

‚Äá6.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Agreement rate is not a neutral metric
The sweep uses agreement rate as a coherence filter ‚ÄĒ configs below 20% agreement are flagged incoherent. But low agreement rate could mean the steered agent is being appropriately firm and walking away from bad deals, not that it's broken. You are potentially discarding your best configs because you've conflated negotiation strategy with model failure.

‚Äá7.‚Ā† ‚Ā†The opening bid is hardcoded at 60% of listing price
In run_game() the buyer always opens at exactly 60% of listing price. This is an arbitrary constant that anchors the entire negotiation. Every result in your sweep is conditional on this single number. If you change it to 50% or 70% your results may look completely different. You have not tested this sensitivity at all.

‚Äá8.‚Ā† ‚Ā†You are only steering the agent's own turns
The steering hook is applied when the steered agent generates its response. But the steered agent also reads the opponent's utterances before responding. Those opponent utterances are not steered and may pull the steered agent's context window in a yielding direction regardless of the hook. The steering is fighting against the context on every turn.

‚Äá9.‚Ā† ‚Ā†max_new_tokens=60 during search is truncating offers
fast_search_steering.py uses max_new_tokens=60 during Stage 1 and 2 to save time. But a natural negotiation response with reasoning plus a DEAL= line can easily exceed 60 tokens. Truncated responses may be missing the DEAL= tag entirely, causing games to time out that would have closed, artificially deflating agreement rates during the search phase and corrupting the config ranking.

10.‚Ā† ‚Ā†You have one model architecture
Everything is tested on Qwen. You have no idea whether any of your results hold on a different architecture. A finding that only replicates on one model family is not a finding ‚ÄĒ it's a property of Qwen. Running even one additional architecture (Llama, Gemma) is the difference between a paper that says "we found X" and a paper that says "we found X on one model."
